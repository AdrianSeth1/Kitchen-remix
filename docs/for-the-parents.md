# Reading your kitchen previews — what to trust

This tool shows you what your kitchen *could* look like with different finishes. It's a real photo of your kitchen with the changes painted in by a computer. Some of what it shows is dependable. Some of it is a friendly guess. Here's how to tell them apart, so you don't make a real decision off a picture the computer made up.

## You can trust these

**New colors and materials.** Cabinets, countertops, backsplash, flooring, and wall paint. When you swap a finish, the computer is only changing the *surface* of things that are already in the photo. It isn't moving anything or inventing anything. If the preview shows sage green cabinets, that's a genuinely useful look at sage green cabinets in *your* kitchen.

**Taking something out.** Removing an object — say, clearing a section of counter or taking out an appliance — is reliable too. The computer just fills the gap with the wall and floor it can already see around it.

**Comparing options side by side.** When you put two or three finishes next to each other, only the finish changes between them. Everything else stays put on purpose, so it's a fair comparison. This is the best way to use the tool: pick a category, line up a few choices, and see which one you like.

## Treat these as a rough impression only

**Moving things around** (like sliding the fridge to a different wall) and **opening or removing a wall.** These are fun to look at, but the computer is *guessing* at sizes, distances, and what's on the other side of a wall it can't see. The app will put a label on these previews reminding you they're approximate.

The picture might look convincing and still be wrong about whether things actually fit. So please:

- **Don't measure off these images.** They are not to scale.
- **Don't decide to knock down a wall** because the preview looked nice. The room it shows beyond the wall is invented, not your real dining room.
- **Use them for the feeling** — "I like the idea of a more open kitchen" — and then have a contractor confirm what's really possible.

## The simple rule

If the change is about **how something looks** (color, material), trust it. If the change is about **where something is** or **how big the room is**, treat it as a sketch and check with a professional before spending money.

When you find finishes you love, use the **spec sheet export** to save them on one page — that's the thing to hand your contractor.
